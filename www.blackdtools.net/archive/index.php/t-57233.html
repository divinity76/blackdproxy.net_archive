Introduction:
Chor is mainly botted by knights and paladins as they are able to withstand physical damage better. Their increased cap helps them to carry several more items as well, which will be needed here. Last bot not least they use "setMeleeKill" instead of "setSpellKill". A knight swings his axe, a paladin throws his spear, while a mage must use alot of mana to kill every lizard. But I bet you are all very aware of why this place is not being botted by mages. As to the point; this script is made for knights and possible for paladins (Not tested but shouldn't be a problem.)

I noticed there isn't alot of bug free/working scripts here at the forum. I see alot of authors promising the script will, for e.g., go deposit at X cap, but (most of) their scripts doesn't actually work. Don't get me wrong, I, as of yet, am unable to make advanced scripts such as depositing gold and stones on X cap perfectly in Edron Goblins. I'm very new to scripting with Black'd and I would definitely not be able to do it any better with my current scripting skills. But any way, I will here release a little script I made for the Ankrahmun/Port Hope Lizard city "Chor". http://tibia.wikia.com/wiki/Chor


IMPORTANT NOTES:
#1. This script does not as of yet contain any depositer. Why? I really don't think there should be a depositer in use when hunting Chor. It's just not "profitable" using so much time walking back to the city as your cap will rapidly decrease when looting heavy items such as plate armors, halberds and obsidian lances.
Instead this script uses a "dropLootOnGround"-command. The bot will go one lap through Chor, and at the end of the waypoints it will go drop items [http://tibia.wikia.com/wiki/Mapper?coords=128.238-128.63-7-4-1-1 here]. This location is safe from being cleaned. I will write how the script works and which items the script drops further down.

#2. This script will in no way get the items dropped on the ground inside a backpack, so you will have to manually throw your loot into backpacks and then manually throwing it back to Ankrahmun, or Port hope, your choice. I recommend bringing a backpack filled with backpacks (1 backpack with 20 backpacks inside of it.) as well as using soft boots, they're really really worth it.

#3. This script does not as of yet contain waypoints to go kill the wyverns close to where the loot is being dropped. You may edit the script yourself if you don't want to wait for me to edit it and upload a newer version with wyvern waypoints. Though the loot list contains all the items that you can loot from wyverns, so you don't have to edit that yourself. Just be aware of what you're doing while editing, remember to edit the "gotoScriptLine X" at the end of the script when adding/deleting lines in the script. (gotoScriptLine X should be set for the line just beneath the comment that says "Arriving at Chor")

#4.There are two different scripts included in this thread. One is called "Ankrahmun - Chor - by Clipster.txt" and loots everything, but the other script called "Ankrahmun - Chor Low Cap - by Clipster.txt" does not loot gold, plate armors, obsidian lances, halberds, templar scytheblades or sentinel shields so that you'll be able to stay there longer, without bothering to throw your loot back when you're leaving.

#5. Not all items are dropped, only items I consider to be of less value/cap. Items such as terra rods, rings, creature products and valuables (e.g. small sapphires) are not dropped as they might be stolen.

As I mentioned above, this script goes one lap, then at the end of each lap goes to drop loot at a certain location. But get this; the script drops loot on four (4) different squares, not just a single one.

Question: I see Chain Armor and Scale Armor is not included, why?
Answer: I excluded looting those to items as they are very heavy and not worth alot. I know every little helps when you want to make alot of money, but there might be laps where you find so many Plate Armors, Steel Helmets and Halberds that you won't be able to carry those Chain- and Scale Armors. So why pick those two items up and risking losing something that's more valuable?

Question: Why doesn't this script use a regular condition such as... IfTrue ($mycap$#number<=#100) Goto X?
Answer: I feel finishing one lap and depositing on the way is far more effective instead of in the middle of a lap running back through everything to drop loot. This will help keeping the spawn relativily cleaned, as this spawn is very crowded, especially in high-populated servers.
